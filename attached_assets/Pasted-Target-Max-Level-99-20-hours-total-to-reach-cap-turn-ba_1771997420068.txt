## 1. High‑level EXP curve concept (Lv 1–99, ~20h total)

You want fast early game, steady mid game, and a grindy but not brutal 80–99.

Assumptions:
- Average pace: 20 hours = 72,000 seconds.
- Average party uses auto‑battle / fast turns, so:
  - ~1 encounter every 15 seconds on average.
  - ~4 encounters per minute ⇒ ~240 encounters per hour.
  - Over 20 hours ≈ 4,800 encounters total (single player grinding).  

Rough design goals:
- Lv 1–40: 4–5 hours total.  
- Lv 41–70: 7–8 hours total.  
- Lv 71–99: 7–9 hours total.  

Sample “EXP to next level” curve (design numbers, not RO exact):
- Lv 1–10: 100 → 1,000 EXP per level (linear).  
- Lv 11–40: grows to ~10,000 EXP per level (almost quadratic).  
- Lv 41–70: grows to ~80,000 EXP per level.  
- Lv 71–98: grows to ~500,000 EXP per level.  
- Lv 98→99: ~1,000,000 EXP (spike, but not insane like original RO). [dbc.reborn](https://dbc.reborn.cz/rodb.cgi?exp=list)

You can express as a function:

- For Level L (2 ≤ L ≤ 99):

  Early (2–10):  
  - EXP(L) ≈ 80 × L^1.4  

  Mid (11–70):  
  - EXP(L) ≈ 120 × L^2  

  Late (71–99):  
  - EXP(L) ≈ 150 × L^2.4  

Then clamp or tweak values by hand to get nice round numbers and hit your “total 20 hours” target.

***

## 2. Expected kills per level and EXP per encounter

If one level takes about 10–20 minutes in early game:

- At ~240 encounters per hour:
  - 10 min ≈ 40 encounters.  
  - 20 min ≈ 80 encounters.  

Design rule:  
- Average “on‑level” player needs **50–80 encounters per level** early, then more later.  

Let:
- `E(L)` = EXP needed from L to L+1.
- `N(L)` = target encounters per level at L (e.g. 60 early, 100 mid, 150 late).

Then:
- Average EXP per encounter at L ≈ E(L) / N(L).  

You can then assign per‑monster EXP around that average (trash slightly below, tank/glass cannon slightly above).

## 3. Early zones (Lv 1–20) – example EXP and HP

Assume:
- Solo player, Lv 1–20 should take around 1.5–2 hours.  
- Encounters: 1–3 monsters.  
- Each encounter should last 2–4 turns.  

### Zone 1: Beginner Area (Lv 1–5)

Target:
- 5–10 minutes to go from Lv 1 to 5.
- Simple mobs, 1–2 per encounter.

Approx EXP to next level (design example):
- Lv 1 → 2: 100  
- Lv 2 → 3: 180  
- Lv 3 → 4: 260  
- Lv 4 → 5: 360  

If you want ~50 encounters total:
- Average ~18–20 EXP per encounter.  

Now define monsters (player basic attack maybe 10–20 damage with 2–3 hits to kill):

Monster roles (Lv ~1–3):

1) Slime  
   - Level: 1  
   - HP: 30  
   - ATK: 3–5  
   - DEF: 0  
   - HIT: tuned so new players hit ~90%  
   - FLEE: low, player is hit ~80–90%  
   - EXP: 6  

2) Small Bat  
   - Level: 2  
   - HP: 40  
   - ATK: 4–6  
   - DEF: 0  
   - FLEE: slightly higher (harder to hit)  
   - EXP: 8  

3) Baby Wolf  
   - Level: 3  
   - HP: 60  
   - ATK: 6–8  
   - DEF: 1  
   - EXP: 12  

Encounter design:
- 1–2 Slimes, sometimes 1 Slime + 1 Bat.  
- Average EXP per encounter (1–2 kills): ≈ 10–20 EXP.  

### Zone 2: Low Plains (Lv 5–10)

Player now has some stats investment and basic gear.

EXP to next level (example):
- Lv 5 → 6: 600  
- Lv 6 → 7: 800  
- Lv 7 → 8: 1,000  
- Lv 8 → 9: 1,300  
- Lv 9 → 10: 1,700  

If we want roughly 60–70 encounters per level:
- Average 20–25 EXP per encounter at 5–7.  
- 25–30 EXP per encounter at 8–10.  

Monster roles (Lv 5–8):

1) Field Poring  
   - Level: 5  
   - HP: 120  
   - ATK: 10–14  
   - DEF: 2  
   - FLEE: low  
   - EXP: 30  

2) Hornet  
   - Level: 6  
   - HP: 100  
   - ATK: 12–16  
   - DEF: 1  
   - FLEE: higher than Poring (dodge build gets value)  
   - EXP: 28  

3) Young Wolf (tankier)  
   - Level: 7  
   - HP: 180  
   - ATK: 14–18  
   - DEF: 3  
   - EXP: 40  

Encounters (1–3 mobs):
- 2 Field Porings  
- 1 Hornet + 1 Poring  
- 1 Young Wolf (mini‑tank fight)  

Expected:
- 2–4 player turns to win fights.  
- 25–50 EXP per encounter (depending on composition).

## 5. Tying in level‑difference EXP modifiers

Use a simple modifier on top of listed monster EXP:

Let:
- `d = monsterLv − playerLv`.

Suggested modifier:
- If |d| ≤ 5 ⇒ 100% EXP.  
- If d ≥ 6:
  - d = 6–10 ⇒ 110%  
  - d = 11+ ⇒ 120% (cap)  
- If d ≤ −6:
  - d = −6 to −10 ⇒ 60%  
  - d = −11 to −20 ⇒ 30%  
  - d ≤ −21 ⇒ 10%  

This keeps your designed EXP/level curve intact while punishing low‑level farming and giving a small bonus to challenging targets. [irowiki](https://irowiki.org/wiki/Experience)

## 6. How to expand this pattern up to 99

1) Decide your EXP per level list using the rough formulas above, then hand‑tune some breakpoints (like RO’s spike near 98–99). [dbc.reborn](https://dbc.reborn.cz/rodb.cgi?exp=list)
2) For each level band (10–20, 20–30, etc.), decide:
   - Expected encounters per level (N(L)).  
   - Baseline EXP per encounter = EXP(L) / N(L).  
3) For each map:
   - Make 3–6 monsters with:
     - HP ≈ 2–5 hits to kill for an on‑level player.  
     - ATK so they take 5–15% of HP on hit.  
   - EXP ≈ baseline, with ±30–50% variation.  
4) Add a few “elite” or “mini‑boss” monsters with:
   - 3–5× HP.  
   - 2× ATK.  
   - 5–10× EXP.
