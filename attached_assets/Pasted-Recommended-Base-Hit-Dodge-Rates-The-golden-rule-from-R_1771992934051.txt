Recommended Base Hit/Dodge Rates
The golden rule from RO's design: a fair fight (same-level player vs same-level enemy) should land ~70–80% of the time. This gives 1-in-5 misses on average — enough to feel meaningful without frustrating the player every turn.
​

Scenario	Hit Chance	Design Intent
Player vs same-level enemy	75%	Default "fair fight" feel 
​
Player vs easy enemy (underlevelled)	90–95%	Grinding feels rewarding and fast
Player vs hard enemy (overlevelled)	45–55%	Danger signal; player needs better gear
Enemy vs Player (default)	65%	Enemy slightly less accurate than player; keeps player agency 
​
Boss vs Player	80–85%	Boss feels threatening; harder to dodge
Why enemy base (65%) is lower than player (75%)? In turn-based RPGs, players need to feel slightly "favored" to stay engaged — if enemies match player accuracy exactly, combat feels punishing rather than skillful.

How to Derive Base Stats
Set your baseline at character creation / Level 1 with these anchor values, then scale with level:

Player Base HIT = 100 + (Level × 2) + (DEX × 1.5)
​

Player Base FLEE = 100 + (Level × 1) + (AGI × 1.5)
​

Enemy HIT = Enemy Level × 3 (simple scaling for monster design)

Enemy FLEE = Enemy Level × 2

Equipment Bonuses: How Much Should Gear Add?
Use small, stackable increments — RO's gear never adds giant leaps in HIT/FLEE, making each upgrade feel meaningful without breaking balance.
​

Equipment Tier	HIT Bonus	FLEE Bonus	Design Rationale
Common (e.g., Iron Sword)	+3–5 HIT	—	Small but noticeable improvement 
​
Uncommon (e.g., Steel Bow)	+8–12 HIT	—	~2–3% hit chance gain per upgrade
Rare/Named (e.g., Hunter's Bow)	+15–20 HIT	+5 FLEE	Meaningful power spike; justifies grind 
​
Legendary	+25–30 HIT	+10–15 FLEE	Endgame cap; used for boss fights
Accessory (e.g., Scope Ring)	+5–10 HIT	—	Secondary slot; stacks with weapon 
​
Light Armor (e.g., Leather Vest)	—	+8–12 FLEE	Evasion-focused build option
Heavy Armor (e.g., Plate Mail)	—	−5 FLEE	Trade-off: more DEF but less dodge 
​
Rule of thumb: Full best-in-slot gear should raise hit chance by at most +15–20% over naked baseline. This prevents gear from making fights trivial; player skill and level still matter most.
