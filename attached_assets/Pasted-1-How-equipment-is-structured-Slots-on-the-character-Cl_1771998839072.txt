1. How equipment is structured
Slots on the character (Classic/pre‑Renewal):

Weapon

Shield (off‑hand)

Armor (body)

Garment (cloak, manteau)

Footgear (shoes, boots)

Accessory 1

Accessory 2

Headgear: Upper / Middle / Lower (three separate head slots)

Each item is just “one piece” that goes into one slot (e.g. “Chain Mail” = body armor, “Poo Poo Hat” = upper headgear). There is no formal item “set bonus” system originally; some sets exist but are hard‑coded per item combination.
​

Design takeaway:

Clear physical slots make it easy for players to understand gear.

You can copy the same layout or reduce it (e.g. 1–2 head slots, 1 accessory slot) if you want simpler gearing.

2. Do they have rarity tiers?
Classic RO does not really use “common / rare / epic / legendary” color tiers like modern MMOs.

Instead, “rarity” is felt through:

Drop rate:

Normal equipment: moderate drop rates (1%–30% depending on item).

“Slot gear” (same armor but with card slot): much rarer drops.
​

Refinement potential:

Some items refine better or have higher base DEF/ATK, so they are more valuable.

Cards:

Cards are very rare (often 0.01% drop). The item itself might be common, but a specific card inside that item becomes extremely “rare” and powerful.

So: rarity is mostly “invisible” (by drop % and slot count), not a colored tier on the UI.

Design takeaway for your game:

You can keep RO’s feel by:

Using drop rate + slot count + cards as the real rarity, without color tiers.

Or you can add visual rarity (colors, borders) on top if you want more modern feedback.

3. What stats do equipment add?
Equipment adds a mix of:

Base defensive/offensive values

Armor: DEF and sometimes MDEF.

Garment: DEF, sometimes FLEE or specific reductions.

Footgear: DEF, Max HP/SP, sometimes movement or FLEE.

Shield: DEF, sometimes racial/size element reductions.

Weapon: ATK (base weapon damage), sometimes MATK on staves.

Flat stat bonuses
Many items give +STR, +AGI, +VIT, +INT, +DEX, +LUK.
Example patterns:

+1~+3 to a single stat

+1 to multiple stats
These feed directly into the formulas you already took (ATK, MATK, HIT, FLEE, etc.).

Percentage modifiers
Some equipment adds multipliers, such as:

“+10% damage to Undead”

“-15% damage from Demi‑Human”

“+10% Max HP”

“+15% walking speed”
These usually apply after base ATK/DEF calculations.

Special effects / conditions
Many items (especially headgears and accessories) do quirky things:

Auto‑cast certain skills when attacking or when being hit.

Increase specific skill levels or reduce SP cost of one skill.

Conditional bonuses: “When HP < 30%, ATK +20%” or “when wearing with X armor, gain Y buff”.

Design takeaway:

Base gear gives ATK/DEF + small stats.

“Interesting” design lives in conditional bonuses and special effects, especially on headgear/accessories.

4. Slot and card system (the real depth)
Each equippable item may have 0–4 “card slots” (shown as,,, in the item name).

Example:

“Chain Mail ” = no slot.

“Chain Mail ” = one card slot.
​

Better gear with slots is very rare and highly valued.
​

Cards:

Dropped by monsters at very low chance.

Each card can be inserted into a slotted item of the right type:

Weapon cards (go into weapons)

Armor cards (body armor)

Shield cards, Garment cards, Footgear cards, Accessory cards, etc.
​

Once inserted, the card is permanent (cannot be removed in official rules).
​

Card effects are usually stronger and more specific than normal gear:

“+15% damage to [Race]”

“+20% damage with [Element] attacks”

“+30% FLEE when HP < 50%”

“+1 to All Stats”

“Ignore DEF of Small enemies”, etc.

Design takeaway:

Base gear defines the skeleton (ATK/DEF/stats).

Cards define the specialization / build (race killer, boss killer, tank, etc.).

Very low drop rate + permanent socket = strong long‑term goals and meaningful choices.
